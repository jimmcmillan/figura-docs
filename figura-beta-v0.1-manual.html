Overview

Figura is an 8-voice polyphonic Inverse Cymatic Synthesiser, an Audio Unit (AUv3) instrument that generates sound from shape, not waveforms. You draw a pattern onto a circular pad, and Figura works out what vibration of a real physical membrane, the same math that governs a drumhead, would produce exactly that pattern. That vibration is the sound: its resonant modes become the synth's oscillator bank.

Two independent shapes (Shape A and Shape B) can be drawn, and a Morph control, with its own LFO, blends continuously between them, so the timbre itself moves as you draw, automate, or modulate.

Installing

Figura ships as a small container app that installs an Audio Unit plugin system-wide.

  • Requirements: macOS, and a host application that supports Audio Unit (AUv3) instrument plugins. Figura is beta software, currently tested on Logic Pro 12.0.1 and Ableton Live 12 Lite.
  • Install: copy Figura.app into your /Applications folder and open it once. This registers the plugin with macOS. You don't need to keep the app open afterwards, and you don't need to use it directly.
  • Finding it in your DAW: look for Figura under the JMac manufacturer name, in your host's Instrument/MIDI-FX AU list.
If Figura doesn't show up in your DAW's plugin list right away, quit and relaunch the DAW. Most hosts only rescan for newly installed Audio Units on launch.

Interface

The main window is organized around the two drawing pads, the Morph control between them, and a live view of the resulting resonance, with all the sound-shaping controls below.

Shape A / Shape B

Two identical drawing pads. Click or tap and drag inside the circle to paint a pattern. Brush strength is additive, so going over the same spot again makes it stronger. Each pad has:

  • Clear: wipes that pad's drawing back to silence.
  • Quick shape buttons: Ring, Star, Cross, Line, Blob, instantly fill the pad with a ready-made pattern, so you can hear the shape-to-sound mapping without freehand drawing.

Morph & Brush

The central Morph knob blends the synth's sound continuously from 100% Shape A to 100% Shape B. Brush sets how large a mark your next stroke makes on either pad.

Plate Resonance / Live Output Spectrum

The right-hand display is a genuine rendering of the vibration your current shape/morph position produces, brighter near the still points (nodes), darker at the points of maximum motion (antinodes), updating live as you draw, morph, or play.

Preset control

The bordered box at the top center always shows the name of the currently loaded preset. Click it to open the Preset Manager; click Save beside it to save your current sound as a new preset. See Presets below for the full picture.

Shaping Sound Through Drawing

Because the whole synth is built on projecting shape onto physical resonance, small changes in where you draw can produce very different-sounding results, more so than most synths' oscillator shapes. A few properties worth knowing:

  • Position matters more than size. A ring drawn near the center of the pad and the same ring drawn near the edge activate almost entirely different resonant modes, because each radius lines up with different natural node lines.
  • Symmetry controls harmonic density. A single off-center dot excites very few modes, simple, clangy, almost pitched. The Star preset deliberately stacks several angular symmetries at once, producing the densest, most inharmonic preset in the factory bank.
  • Morphing while playing is the most direct way to feel this. Draw two very different shapes into A and B, then move Morph (by hand, by automation, or via the LFO) while holding a note. The timbre visibly and audibly transforms in step with the shapes.

Presets & the Preset Manager

Click the preset name at the top of the window to open the full-screen Preset Manager: Figura's own preset browser, organized into banks.

Banks

Factory (locked) User + your own custom banks

Factory is read-only and ships with 11 presets: Init, Ring, Star, Cross, Line, Blob, plus five hand-designed extras. Frantic Filtered Ring to Star, Hover Car Racer, Mystic Flutter, Slow Morphing Ring to Star, and Spooky Chime. User is where anything you save lands by default, and you can create as many additional banks as you like to organize your own patches, with New Bank / Rename Bank / Delete Bank controls right in the manager.

Loading a preset

Single-click a preset to load and audition it. Double-click to load it and close the manager in one action.

Saving your own sounds

Save

Overwrites the currently selected User/custom-bank preset in place with your current sound. The fast way to update a patch you're iterating on.

Save New

Prompts for a name and saves your current sound as a brand-new preset. This is also what the Save button next to the preset name in the main window does, jumping straight to the User bank so you can name and store it in one step.

Import & Export

Figura's presets are plain .aupreset files, the same format Logic and Ableton use natively. So they're fully interchangeable:

  • Export / Import (single preset): Save any one preset out to a file, or bring any .aupreset file in as a new User preset.
  • Export Bank / Import Bank: Export every preset in a bank as a folder in one go, or import a whole folder of .aupreset files as a new bank.
Figura's own Preset Manager and your host's native preset menu work side by side. Factory presets appear in both.

Tips & Things to Try

  • A single small dot, off-center, gives a simple, almost-pitched, clangy tone, a good starting point for learning how shape maps to sound.
  • Draw a thin ring near the center of the pad, listen, then Clear and redraw it near the edge. The timbre shifts in discrete steps between the two, since each radius lines up with different resonant modes.
  • Try wildly different shapes in A and B, then set LFO Depth high with a slow Rate for a constantly evolving pad sound.
  • Use a fast Filter Envelope with negative Env Amount and a short Amp decay for percussive, plate-like plucks.
  • Duplicate a Factory preset into User first if you want to tweak it. Factory stays untouched as your reference.

Troubleshooting

  • Figura doesn't appear in my DAW's plugin list. Make sure you've opened Figura.app at least once, then restart your DAW. Most hosts only scan for new Audio Units on launch.
  • Where are my saved presets stored? Inside Figura's own app data. They persist across app updates and don't need to be backed up manually, but Export (or Export Bank) is the safest way to keep an external copy of anything important.
  • A preset I imported doesn't show up. Only files with a .aupreset extension are recognized as valid preset files.
